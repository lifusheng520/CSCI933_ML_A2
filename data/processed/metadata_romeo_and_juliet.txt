PLAY TITLE: Romeo and Juliet

DESCRIPTION:
A celebrated tragedy set in Verona, Italy, depicting the ill-fated love between two young people from feuding noble families. It explores themes of passionate love, fate, and the destructive nature of generational hatred.

MAIN STORY THREAD:
- The ancient feud between the Montague and Capulet families frequently erupts into violence in Verona.
- Romeo Montague and Juliet Capulet meet at a ball and fall instantly in love.
- With the help of Friar Laurence, they marry in secret, hoping to bridge the gap between their families.
- A series of fatal misunderstandings and violent encounters leads to the exile of Romeo.
- Juliet fakes her death to escape an arranged marriage and reunite with Romeo.
- Due to a failed message, Romeo believes Juliet is truly dead and takes his own life at her tomb.
- Upon waking and finding Romeo dead, Juliet kills herself.
- The grieving families finally agree to end their feud.

ROLE MATRIX:
- Romeo: The son of Montague; an impulsive and romantic young man.
- Juliet: The daughter of Capulet; a courageous and devoted young woman.
- Friar Laurence: A Franciscan monk who secretly marries the lovers, hoping for peace.
- Mercutio: Romeo's witty and mercurial friend; a kinsman to the Prince.
- Tybalt: Juliet's hot-tempered cousin; a Capulet who loathes the Montagues.
- The Nurse: Juliet's faithful confidante and maternal figure.
- Lord/Lady Capulet & Lord/Lady Montague: The heads of the feuding households.
- Count Paris: A kinsman of the Prince and the suitor preferred by Juliet’s parents.
